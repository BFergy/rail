DESTINATIONS






 

Why CONRAIL?

My fictional railroad was something that had been slowly simmering in my head since as far back as the 1980s. The rationale behind the railroad dictated that it would be located in south-central Ontario, and very early on I had selected a name for it - the Central Ontario Rail System. I have always liked the blue and white livery employed by the real CONRAIL so it was a no-brainer (always a good thing for me) that their equipment would form the basis for my freelance home road.

 

The Underlying Concept

Like many freelanced model railroads, my Central Ontario Rail System is based on a 'what if' scenario.

I live about 1.5 miles from the former CP Owen Sound subdivision. The line was built between 1868 and 1873 by the Toronto, Grey and Bruce Railway and ran from Orangeville to Owen Sound (about 71 miles). CP took over the line in the 1940s and, for profitability reasons, it was officially abandoned in 1995 and the rails were soon removed. The line is now a rail trail.

But what if operation of the line had been taken over by another railway? And what if that railway was not only able to make the line profitable but was also able to expand and serve an entirely new group of freight customers? That's my fictional concept for CONRAIL and I have chosen to model a portion of the new railroad's Southgate subdivision.

 

The Southgate Subdivision

The Southgate sub is the southern portion of the former Owen Sound sub, terminating at a small yard near Dundalk in the township of Southgate, while the line continues north on the remaining portion of the Owen Sound sub.

The Southgate yard directly serves four nearby active customers and has a small engine facility. CONRAIL interchanges with CP at Streetsville and both CN and Ontario Southland at Guelph.

While CP does not venture onto the Southgate sub, it is not uncommon to see both CN and OSR power in the Southgate yard since CONRAIL has reciprocal agreements with both.
