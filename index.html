About this site

This site is NOT mobile friendly! It may work (sort of) in landscape mode, depending on your device. A PC or laptop is the best option.

Back in the dark ages, I created several web sites that were considered pretty good at the time. But well over 25 years have passed since then. This is merely a clone of an old site I built as I have no interest in learning newer web development methods.

In essence, I authored these pages while waiting for paint or glue to dry, or scenery materials to harden on my layout. If you're looking for entertainment there are far better places to find it!

But if you're bored, the destination links at the left will transport you to pages where I demonstrate my lack of skill and knowledge.

Happy railroading!

 

About Me

Not that you want to know but I will babble a bit about myself anyway. I got my first model train set over six decades ago. It was an O-scale, 3-rail, tinplate set with 0-4-0 steam engine, tender, 3 cars, and a caboose. I was about 5 or 6 at the time and it was a Christmas present - I loved it!

I had grandparents who lived on the east side of London, Ontario and their house was within sight of the CP mainline. I loved visiting there!

By the time I was 10 or so, I had a small HO layout, set up on a 4x8 table my dad had built just for that purpose. The highlight of that was a CN F7 unit in green and gold.

My teen years put a long-term pause in my interest in model trains. Cars, car racing, girls, etc. took over. I still loved trains but they weren't a priority.

At about the age of 30, I saw some N-scale Micro-trains stuff and the bug bit me again. I built a small layout in a spare room in my house. But that only lasted for 2 or 3 years, until I got married.

I was a computer software programmer for many years, until I got tired of the corporate system I found myself in. I began working for myself as a freelance programmer during a time when companies were outsourcing. But that eventually came to an end as more and more companies brought that work back in-house. On a recommendation from my brother, I ended up working for CN Rail, finally as a mobile crane operator at their Brampton intermodal yard, where I eventually retired in 2018.

And the model railroad interest returned. I began researching and acquiring HO models in 2018. But it wasn't until 2022 that I got serious about having some sort of layout.

I'm old now, slow, overthink everything, and progress happens at a snail's pace. But hey, I'm having fun!
