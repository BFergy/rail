DESTINATIONS





 

Reality Crushes Dreams

Like so many modellers I had always envisioned a rather large, sprawling layout with multiple trains running, extensive scenery, numerous industries, and a very large rail yard. But, as it often does, reality gave me a wake-up call and forced me to decide between building what I could afford, in the space I had available, or just keep dreaming of the ultimate railroad that would never be realized.

With my 70th birthday already behind me, I decided that building anything was better than having nothing at all.

For a variety of reasons, I had to settle for a rather simple shelf layout. It could utilize three walls of a bedroom, totalling around 27 feet in length and have a maximum width of 20 inches. Furniture dictated a shelf height of 56-57 inches.

 

Designing

After a few frustrating attempts at designing a layout with pencil and paper, I gave in to the voices in my head and downloaded the AnyRail software. After playing a bit with the free version, I quickly bought the full version. It was money well spent.

It didn't take long to decide that I could include a small yard and about four industries. The latter were predetermined as I already had the building kits. Background buildings might have been a better choice but I am making do with what I have.

I was also determined to incorporate a few small scenic elements where possible.

It took me a bit of time to learn the AnyRail software but I highly recommend it, or a similarly capable package, for layout design. Unlike pencil and paper, you know exactly where everything will be - I had no nasty surprises once I started building. You can also create many versions of your plan quite simply and compare them to achieve the best design. The software includes libraries for many common track brands (as well as many structure kit footprints), and the Peco Code 83 pieces were dead-on accurate. By printing off selected sections of the plan at full-scale size, it becomes very easy to transfer critical track sections to the subroadbed on the layout.

Regardless of what method you use to design a layout, commit plenty of time to the planning process. It may feel like you are wasting time because you just want to get started on the actual construction, but you will more than gain that time back later, encountering far fewer mistakes, avoiding many headaches, and ultimately enjoying the finished layout far more.

 

Final AnyRail Plan

 

One of AnyRail's handy features is the ability to draw on separate layers. You can turn the visibility of a layer on and off, so the visible drawing is never cluttered. For convenience, I built the shelf in four separate pieces, with ladder-style construction. To ensure that shelf cross-members did not interfere with turnouts (all use switch machines), I drew suitable locations for them on a separate layer. Using the ruler function, I then transfered those locations to the shelf.
